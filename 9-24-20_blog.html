Designing webpages with HTML/CSS allows the creator to fine tune any aspect of the site with seemingly limitless design options. While many websites are designed with the user in mind, not everyone will be pleased with the site or appreciate the design. Allowing the webpage visitor to have some control over minor aspects of page design can ensure that everyone will enjoy a website, since it is tailored specifically for their own experience. Giving the option to change the background color of text on the page or the page itself allows the user to feel they have some control over their experience and increases positivity towards the site.

Many programmers are not thrilled with the idea of giving any sort of control over to a user, as it tends to cause many new problems to arise. Using select and option tags in HTML are a fun, safe way of giving users the ability to edit a portion of the page without compromising the developers hard work. Drop down menus can be created in HTML with color options that a user may click to decide the perfect background color that will complement their viewing experience. This interesting aspect of HTML is a great way to make the user feel important and gives them a small look into the world of webpage design.

Example Code for Changing Background Color:

        <select name="clr" onchange="document.bgColor=this.options[this.selectedIndex].value" size="1">
            <br>
            <option value="black">black
            <option value="orange">orange
            <option value="flamingred">fuschia
            <option value="lightyellow">light yellow
            <option value="green">green
            <option value="cyan">cyan
            <option value="yellow">yellow
            <option value="red">red
            <option value="white">white</option>
        </select>  Background Color
